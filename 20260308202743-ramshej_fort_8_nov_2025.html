  7. Region: Near Nashik, on the Peth road side; a classic Sahyadri fort.[web:12][web:15]
  8. Grade: Moderate; noticeable steepness near the top, but manageable with breaks.
  9. Route: Base village → trail through fields and rocky patches → carved steps/rock paths → fort entrance → bastions and top plateau with views.
  10. Trek time: ~2–3 hrs up, ~1.5–2 hrs down.
  11. Access: Popular from Nashik, often done as the “grand finale” of a local trek series.
  12. Best season: Post-monsoon to winter; summer climbs are hot and exposed.
  13. Water/food: Carry full water; check if any seasonal stalls operate at base.
  14. Safety: Final sections have some exposure; small, controlled steps help, especially on descent.
  15. Fact: Ramshej has well-known links to Maratha–Mughal conflicts and is part of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj’s fort network.[web:12]
  16. Quote: “On Ramshej, the view feels less like scenery and more like a history book opening.”
