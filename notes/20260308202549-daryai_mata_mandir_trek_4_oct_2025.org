- Region: Dari Matori area, near Makhmalabad–Nashik belt.[web:32]
- Grade: Easy; short morning trek plus valley/temple visit.
- Route: Small approach path from road/parking → gradual trail towards the temple area → viewpoints over valley, stream sections in season.
- Trek time: ~1.5–2.5 hrs including photo and rest stops.
- Access: Short drive from Nashik; popular with local groups as a “small trek but full experience.”
- Best season: Monsoon and post-monsoon for water flow and lush greenery; winter for clear skies.
- Water/food: Carry your own water and a tiffin; sometimes villagers sell tea/snacks, but not guaranteed.
- Safety: Rocks near water get slippery; watch footing around streams and edges; keep group together as paths branch.
- Fact: The combination of small temple, moving water and valley views makes it feel like a full trek “compressed” into a short distance.
- Quote: “Daryai Mata proves you don’t need a huge altitude gain to feel a big shift inside.”
