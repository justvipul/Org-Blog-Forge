** Kalsubai Peak – 29 Dec 2024
- Region: Kalsubai–Harishchandragad Wildlife Sanctuary, Ahmednagar district.
- Height: 1646 m / 5400 ft (highest peak of Maharashtra).
- Grade: Moderate; long climb with iron ladders, suitable for fit beginners.
- Route: Standard Bari village route; fields → gradual slope → multiple iron ladders → summit temple plateau.
- Trek time: ~3–4 hrs ascent, 2–3 hrs descent; total 6–7 hrs with breaks.
- Access: Drive/bus to Bari from Nashik via Ghoti–Bhandardara side; paid parking in village.
- Best season: Oct–Feb for views; monsoon for clouds (more slippery).
- Water/food: Carry 2–3 L water; stalls with tea/poha often present in season, but don’t depend fully.
- Safety: Start pre-dawn for sunrise; ladders can jam on weekends; rock & metal are very slippery in rain.
- Fact: From the top you can see Harishchandragad, Ratangad and other major Sahyadri peaks on a clear day.
- Quote: “Climbing Kalsubai doesn’t just raise your altitude, it quietly raises your standards for what you can do.”

** Dehergad (Bhorgad) Fort – 2025
- Region: Near Nashik, in the Peth range of the Western Ghats.[web:15]
- Height: ~1000 m / ~3297 ft (approx).
- Grade: Easy–Moderate; mostly forested trail with a final rocky section near the fort structures.
- Route: Forest entry → gentle jungle trail → a couple of steeper forested climbs → open ridge with views → ruined fort walls/structure.[web:10]
- Trek time: 2–3 hrs ascent, 1.5–2 hrs descent.
- Access: From Nashik by road towards Peth region; local guidance is very helpful because of similar-looking forest tracks.[web:15]
- Best season: Post-monsoon to winter; monsoon makes the forest lush but also more leech-prone and slippery.
- Water/food: No reliable water on trail; carry full requirement from base (2–3 L); food only in nearby villages, so tiffin is best.
- Safety: Forest sections can be confusing; stay with group and avoid venturing off-track; watch for loose soil and leaf-covered stones.
- Fact: Dehergad/Bhorgad area is part of a reforestation and biodiversity recovery story where local efforts helped bring back green cover and wildlife.[web:7][web:11]
- Quote: “Some forts roar with crowds, others like Dehergad whisper through trees and broken stones.”

** Borgad Jungle Safari – 11 Oct 2025
- Region: Near Nashik, reforested hill and forest area.[web:7][web:11]
- Height: ~3480 ft (approx summit ridge).
- Grade: Easy; usually done as a jungle walk/safari rather than a summit push.
- Route: Entry point → forest track → viewpoints over reforested slopes and small water bodies; specific paths vary by guide and permission.
- Access: Close to Nashik city; access only through organised groups and with special permission due to proximity to restricted areas.
- Best season: Post-monsoon for full green cover.
- Water/food: Treat as a half-day walk but still carry water and light snacks.
- Safety: Area touches sensitive/IAF-linked zones; always respect boundaries, follow guides, and avoid off-trail wandering.
- Fact: Around 1.5–2.5 lakh trees have been planted here over years as part of major afforestation drives, transforming the hill’s biodiversity.[web:7][web:11]
- Quote: “Borgad is a reminder that forests can be destroyed by people—and also rebuilt by people.”

** Panjrapole Chunchele Farm Jungle Safari – 20 Sep 2025
- Region: Shree Nasik Panchavati Panjrapole farm area near Chunchale, Nashik.[web:35]
- Grade: Easy safari-style outing; done by vehicle plus short walks.
- Area: ~850 acres of land with trees, water bodies, grazing land and farm sections (as mentioned in your notes).
- Route: Internal farm/forest tracks by tractor/jeep; short walks to view cattle, birds, small water bodies and plantation areas.
- Access: Only with prior permission from Nasik Panchavati Panjrapole; not an open public trekking trail.
- Best season: Post-monsoon to winter for pleasant weather and greenery.
- Water/food: Typically includes organised snacks/tea; still carry a bottle and personal essentials.
- Safety: Respect animals’ space; do not litter or feed without permission; this is a working gaushala and agri-tourism site.
- Fact: The farm hosts hundreds of cattle and a mix of horticulture, composting, and other agri-based projects, blending animal care with green initiatives.[web:35]
- Quote: “Not every jungle is wild—some are carefully grown, one sapling and one animal at a time.”

** Daryai Mata Mandir Trek – 4 Oct 2025
- Region: Dari Matori area, near Makhmalabad–Nashik belt.[web:32]
- Grade: Easy; short morning trek plus valley/temple visit.
- Route: Small approach path from road/parking → gradual trail towards the temple area → viewpoints over valley, stream sections in season.
- Trek time: ~1.5–2.5 hrs including photo and rest stops.
- Access: Short drive from Nashik; popular with local groups as a “small trek but full experience.”
- Best season: Monsoon and post-monsoon for water flow and lush greenery; winter for clear skies.
- Water/food: Carry your own water and a tiffin; sometimes villagers sell tea/snacks, but not guaranteed.
- Safety: Rocks near water get slippery; watch footing around streams and edges; keep group together as paths branch.
- Fact: The combination of small temple, moving water and valley views makes it feel like a full trek “compressed” into a short distance.
- Quote: “Daryai Mata proves you don’t need a huge altitude gain to feel a big shift inside.”

** Mayna Parvat (Gavlawadi, Makhmalabad) – 18 Oct 2025
- Region: Near Makhmalabad, Nashik; part of local hill line you see from the city.[web:12][web:15]
- Grade: Easy–Moderate; straightforward climb but with steady incline.
- Route: Base near Gavlawadi/Makhmalabad → gradual trail on grassy slopes → more defined path near the top → temple and open summit area; descent often via a slightly different jungle path for variety.
- Trek time: ~1.5–2 hrs up, ~1–1.5 hrs down, plus picnic time at top.
- Access: Short drive from Nashik; very convenient “any weekend” trek.
- Best season: Post-monsoon; in winter it becomes a great practice and “conditioning” hill.
- Water/food: No guaranteed water on trail; carry 2 L and snacks; your group often does summit picnic.
- Safety: In jungle descent, always keep one leg firmly placed and test next foothold, especially on loose soil and leaves.
- Fact: This is your “story-rich” trek where you met a 100-year-old man living alone on the hill and shared food and money with him, turning the hike into a life lesson.[conversation_history:8]
- Quote: “Some summits give views, Mayna Parvat gave me a person I’ll never forget.”

** Sula Dongar – 25 Oct 2025
- Region: Near Sula / Makhmalabad side of Nashik.[web:12][web:15]
- Grade: Easy; short half-day hill.
- Route: Local approach path → gradual ridge → open top with views towards vineyards and nearby hills.
- Trek time: ~1.5–2 hrs up and down total.
- Access: Very close to Nashik; best done as a quick morning trek.
- Best season: Post-monsoon for green vineyards and clear views; winter for crisp air.
- Water/food: Carry basic water and snacks; no formal facilities on top.
- Safety: Grass slopes can hide uneven stones; monsoon mud can be slick.
- Fact: This was your first trek after Diwali that year, making it feel like a “fresh start” hill.
- Quote: “After the noise of Diwali, Sula Dongar was like a reset button made of wind and sky.”

** Chamar Leni – 1 Nov 2025
- Region: Near Nashik, accessible via Peth road side; cave complex on a hill.[web:12][web:15]
- Grade: Easy–Moderate; mix of steps, path and some steeper bits.
- Route: Base near road → steps/path up → caves and temple structures → further path to top plateau area used by trekkers for practice.
- Trek time: 2–3 hrs overall including exploring caves.
- Access: From Nashik via Peth road; used often as a practice trek for tougher climbs like Pandav Leni rounds.
- Best season: Year-round, but post-monsoon and winter are most pleasant.
- Water/food: Carry your own; occasional local stalls near base depending on day.
- Safety: Watch steps when crowded or wet; carry a headlamp if exploring darker cave corners.
- Fact: The site has religious and historic significance, and for you it also became a “training ground” after Pandav Leni.
- Quote: “Chamar Leni is where rock, faith and fitness quietly shake hands.”

** Ramshej Fort – 8 Nov 2025
- Region: Near Nashik, on the Peth road side; a classic Sahyadri fort.[web:12][web:15]
- Grade: Moderate; noticeable steepness near the top, but manageable with breaks.
- Route: Base village → trail through fields and rocky patches → carved steps/rock paths → fort entrance → bastions and top plateau with views.
- Trek time: ~2–3 hrs up, ~1.5–2 hrs down.
- Access: Popular from Nashik, often done as the “grand finale” of a local trek series.
- Best season: Post-monsoon to winter; summer climbs are hot and exposed.
- Water/food: Carry full water; check if any seasonal stalls operate at base.
- Safety: Final sections have some exposure; small, controlled steps help, especially on descent.
- Fact: Ramshej has well-known links to Maratha–Mughal conflicts and is part of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj’s fort network.[web:12]
- Quote: “On Ramshej, the view feels less like scenery and more like a history book opening.”

** Pandav Leni – 15 Nov 2025
- Region: On the outskirts of Nashik city; famous rock-cut caves and steps.[web:12]
- Grade: Easy if done once; Moderate if you do multiple rounds for training.
- Route: Well-built steps and path from base parking → cave complex → further path to top and circular trail on the plateau.
- Trek time: 45–90 minutes for one full up-down circuit; you often repeat multiple times a year as “default trek.”
- Access: Extremely easy from Nashik; city landmark.
- Best season: All year; early mornings best to beat heat and crowds.
- Water/food: Shops and water at base; carry your own bottle for the climb.
- Safety: Steps can be crowded and slippery in rain; keep pace comfortable and avoid racing others.
- Fact: Pandav Leni is your “whenever no plan, go trek” spot and a core part of your yearly fitness.
- Quote: “When I have no plan, I have Pandav Leni.”

** Gadgad Sangvi – 22 Nov 2025
- Region: Near Nashik; accessed from Decathlon / highway side as per your group notes.
- Grade: Easy–Moderate; new ridge trail explored with guidance.
- Route: Start from road-side point → village tracks → hill trail → ridge with sunrise views → return same way.
- Trek time: Half-day morning trek; out and back.
- Access: Short drive from Nashik; route best done with someone who knows the new trail.
- Best season: Post-monsoon for green ridges and misty views.
- Water/food: Bring all your water and snacks; no formal facilities on top.
- Safety: Newer trail means fewer signboards; stay close to group and avoid splitting.
- Fact: This trek was guided by a local (Nana Gaydhani etc.) and quickly became a favourite “new discovery” in your list.
- Quote: “Every year deserves at least one trail where your map is your friends’ footsteps.”

** Raygadnagar Tekdi – 6 Dec 2025
- Region: Near Raigad Nagar / RPF firing range belt outside Nashik.
- Grade: Easy; intentionally kept as a short family trek.
- Route: Short hill path from selected starting point → small top with dam view (Waldevi side) → back the same route.
- Trek time: ~1.5–2 hrs total including enjoying the view.
- Access: Close to Nashik; sensitive area due to firing range – exact route details are best kept offline and within trusted groups.
- Best season: Clear winter mornings for best dam views.
- Water/food: Carry a bottle; breakfast plan was at a Jain temple on your group trek.
- Safety: Respect firing range boundaries and all instructions; this is not a random wander hill.
- Fact: This tekdi is more about safe, small practice climbs and views than about height or difficulty.
- Quote: “Some hills are not about adventure; they are about learning how to move in sensitive places with respect.”

** Ghalna Fort – 21 Dec 2025
- Region: Near Malegaon, north-east of Nashik, in the Satmala / adjoining range.[web:14]
- Height: ~1000+ m class fort (similar to many mid-sized Sahyadri forts).[web:14]
- Grade: Moderate; good path but sustained climb, with historic gateways and structures en route.
- Route: Ghalna village → trail from Gorakshanath/Shivpanchayatan temple at base → climb to first stone gateway (parkoat) with carvings → further gateways including iron gate and later “Lakhya Darwaza” style entrance → top plateau with mosque, hause (water tanks), ruined structures and remains of Ambarkhana and offices.[web:10][web:14]
- Trek time: ~1.5–2 hrs ascent depending on crowd; similar for descent; your event day included long exploration and demonstrations.
- Access: From Nashik via Malegaon road, then internal rural road to Ghalna village; your trek was a big organised event with 6 buses and ~300 trekkers.
- Best season: Post-monsoon to winter; summer heats the rock heavily.
- Water/food: Carry full day’s water (4–5 L as advised by organisers) and tiffin; no dependable facilities on top.
- Safety: Big groups must be disciplined at gateways and narrow sections; crowd control and sticking to marked path is important.
- History/fact: Ghalna’s multiple carved gateways, Persian inscription, mosque and large tanks reflect its layered history across Sultanate, Mughal and British periods.[web:10][web:14]
- Personal note: This was part trek, part cultural event with “gadadhari” demonstrations, Shivaji-era martial vibes, and a strong sense of shared history.
- Quote: “On Ghalna, every gateway feels like a checkpoint between centuries.”
