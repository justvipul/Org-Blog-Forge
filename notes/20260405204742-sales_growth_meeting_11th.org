* 1: Opening (2 min)
** Daily sales growth comes from temperament, not just talent
- Today’s 3 ideas:
  - Hanuman complex
  - Munger’s discipline
  - Sales patience and self-control

* 2: Hanuman complex (6 min)
** Capability exists, but action gets blocked
- Hanuman is strong and capable.
- Jambavan does not teach new skills.
- He reminds Hanuman of what he already is.
- That reminder changes action.
- Sales lesson:
  - We often know the answer but hesitate.
  - We forget our past competence.
  - We need evidence, not mood.

* 3: Sales parallel (4 min)
** What this looks like in sales
- Knowing what to say but not saying it.
- Delaying a follow-up even after a good lead.
- Deferring decisions even when capable.
- Feeling less competent than actual ability.
- Fix:
  - Recall prior success.
  - Act before full emotional certainty.
  - Track hesitation as a pattern.

* 4: Munger mindset (6 min)
** Simple, patient, rational
- Keep it simple.
- Sit and wait for good opportunities.
- Be a little wiser every day.
- Use checklists.
- Learn from mistakes.
- Invert: ask what can go wrong first.

* 5: Temperament in sales (4 min)
** What matters more than raw intelligence
- Patience.
- Discipline.
- Emotional control.
- Ability to wait.
- Ability to recover from rejection.
- Sales is a long game.
- Don’t confuse motion with progress.

* 6: Closing takeaways (3 min)
** What I want the team to remember
- Capability is already inside us.
- Confidence can be restored by action.
- Great salespeople are consistent, simple, patient, and alert.
- Use checklists and reflection.
- Improve slowly, every day.

* 7: One-line challenge (5 min)
** This week’s action
- Each person should identify:
  - 1 deal where they hesitated.
  - 1 thing they already knew but did not trust.
  - 1 action they will take earlier next time.
