*  1: Opening (2 min)
** What this meeting is about
- Today’s theme is simple: how to act with more confidence, more patience, and more discipline in sales.
- We will connect three ideas:
  - Hanuman complex.
  - Charlie Munger’s wisdom.
  - Practical habits for sales performance.
- The goal is not theory; the goal is better action today.

*  2: Hanuman Complex (5 min)
** Strength exists before confidence returns
- Hanuman had the power already.
- Jambavan did not give him new skills.
- He simply reminded Hanuman of what he was capable of.
- This is a useful sales idea:
  - Sometimes we are not weak, we are temporarily disconnected from our own ability.
  - We hesitate even when we already know what to do.
  - The solution is often not more information, but a reminder of prior competence.
- In sales, this looks like:
  - knowing the next step but delaying it.
  - doubting our own judgment despite past wins.
  - waiting for full certainty before acting.
- Reference note:
  - This idea is described in the Hanuman complex psychotherapy article, where Hanuman regains self-awareness when reminded by Jambavan.[web:67][web:71]

*  3: Sales Meaning (4 min)
** What this means for us
- We often forget what we are capable of, even when evidence says otherwise.
- That hesitation is not always a skill problem; sometimes it is a belief problem.
- Use evidence, not mood.
- Recall prior wins, past recoveries, and earlier difficult deals you handled well.
- Action can restore confidence faster than overthinking.

*  4: Munger Wisdom (5 min)
** Quotes to guide sales thinking
- “We have a passion for keeping things simple.”
- “The big money is not in buying or selling, but in the waiting.”
- “You don’t have to be brilliant, only a little bit wiser than the other guys, on average, for a long, long time.”
- “No wise pilot, no matter how great his talent and experience, fails to use a checklist.”
- “There is no way you can live an adequate life without making mistakes.”
- “Invert, always invert.”
- “A majority of life’s errors are caused by forgetting what one is really trying to do.”
- These lines support three habits:
  - Simplicity.
  - Patience.
  - Self-correction.

*  5: Sales Discipline (3 min)
** What we should do daily
- Do not chase every opportunity.
- Wait for real prospects and real signals.
- Use checklists.
- Learn from mistakes quickly.
- Think backward:
  - What can ruin the deal?
  - What can ruin trust?
  - What can ruin speed?
- A strong sales person is not only active, but also careful.

*  6: Team Message (1 min)
** One-line reminder
- We do not need to be the smartest in every room.
- We need to be more disciplined, more honest, and more consistent than yesterday.

*  7: Closing action (0.5 min)
** One action for today
- Each person should identify:
  - one deal where they hesitated.
  - one thing they already knew but did not trust.
  - one action they will take earlier next time.

*  8: Closing note (0.5 min)
** Final thought
- Capability is often already inside us.
- The job is to remember it, trust it, and act on it.
