Slide 1: Title

Daily Sales Growth via Human Psychology & Better Thinking

  • The Purchase House – Used Machine e-Marketplace
  • Speaker: Chief Sales Coordinator
  • Goal: How to sell more, think better, and stay mentally tough – even without technical background

Slide 2: Context – Why This Session?

Our Reality at The Purchase House

  • Huge variety of CNC / laser / press brake machines
  • Team is non‑technical; even engineers need time to understand specs
  • Result:
    • Confusion → hesitation → lost deals
    • Focus on “features” instead of customer problems
  • Today’s focus:
    • Use human psychology + simple analysis instead of deep technical knowledge

Slide 3: From Features to Human Psychology

People Decide Emotionally, Justify Rationally

  • Customers don’t buy “machines”; they buy:
    • Fewer breakdowns
    • Faster delivery
    • Risk reduction
    • Respect / status in their factory
  • Key idea (from Seeking Wisdom):
    • Understand how people misjudge and how to avoid our own misjudgments

Slide 4: 4 Psychological Levers in Sales & Negotiation

  1. Reciprocity – people return favours
  2. Social proof – people follow others in similar situations
  3. Commitment & consistency – once they say “yes” to small things, they like to stay consistent
  4. Loss aversion – people fear losses more than they value gains

(We will connect each to used‑machine sales.)

Slide 5: Reciprocity – Give First, Then Ask

How to Use in Our Sales

  • Before pushing for PO, create value:
    • Share a simple comparison:
      • “Here are 2–3 options by budget and job type”
    • Send a quick video explaining key difference (rpm, bed size, power) in simple language
    • Share one practical tip:
      • e.g. “How to inspect a used machine in 10 minutes”
  • WhatsApp examples:
    • “Sir, I made a small 1‑page summary for you: [PDF / image]”
    • “Here is a machine inspection checklist – free, use with any dealer”

Slide 6: Social Proof – “People Like You Buy This”

Remove Risk by Showing Others

  • Buyers ask internally: “Have similar companies bought this?”
  • Use:
    • Stories: “Last month a Pune job‑shop with 6 VMCs chose this machine because…”
    • Numbers: “We have placed 12 similar machines in Maharashtra in last 6 months”
  • On WhatsApp:
    • Before/after photos
    • Short testimonial audio/text from happy client (with permission)

Slide 7: Consistency – Get Small “Yes” First

Step‑by‑Step Commitment

  • Don’t jump to PO. Move client through small commitments:
    1. “Can I WhatsApp you 2–3 options with pros/cons?” → Yes
    2. “Can we do a 10‑minute video call to see the machine live?” → Yes
    3. “If this fits, can we plan an inspection on Friday?” → Yes
  • Once they say “yes” repeatedly, saying “no” to order becomes psychologically harder

Slide 8: Loss Aversion – Sell by Highlighting Avoided Pain

Customers Hate Losses More Than They Love Gains

  • Examples of pains:
    • Machine breakdown during urgent order
    • Scrap and rework
    • Unplanned maintenance cost
  • Talk in “loss avoided” language:
    • “This model reduces downtime risk because parts are easily available”
    • “This dealer gives proper service history – saves you surprise repair bills”

Slide 9: Negotiation – Human Psychology, Not Drama

Simple Negotiation Concepts

  • BATNA (Best Alternative To a Negotiated Agreement):
    • Know our walk‑away point: price, credit terms, delivery time
  • Win‑win frame:
    • “Let’s see how we can fit your budget and still protect your production risk”
  • Psychological tactics to avoid:
    • Bluffing about non‑existent buyers
    • Last‑minute emotional pressure
  • We want long‑term repeat customers, not one‑time wins

Slide 10: WhatsApp as Our Main Weapon

Best Practices for Non‑Technical Team

  • Clean profile:
    • DP = logo / professional photo
    • Status = short line: “Used CNC / Laser / Press Brake – The Purchase House”
  • Message structure:
    1. Context: “Sir, as discussed – machine options for mild steel cutting”
    2. Options: 2–3 short bullets per machine, no jargon
    3. CTA: “Shall we inspect Option 2 on Thursday 3 PM?”
  • Use:
    • Broadcast lists for updates (not random spam)
    • Quick voice notes when text is too long

Slide 11: Using AI to Save Time (Non‑Technical Friendly)

Practical AI Use‑Cases for Us

  • 1) Drafting messages & proposals:
    • Input: “Client wants 2m x 4m laser, 6–8mm mild steel, budget 30–35L, write a short proposal”
  • 2) Clarifying technical terms:
    • “Explain difference between CO2 laser and fiber laser to a non‑technical buyer in 5 points”
  • 3) Preparing comparison sheets:
    • “Compare Machine A vs Machine B for SME job shop – focus: maintenance, space, power, accuracy”
  • 4) Translating:
    • English ↔ Hindi/Marathi for local comfort
  • Golden rule:
    • AI drafts, you verify & customise

Slide 12: Simple Analysis – Think Like Munger

Before Pushing a Machine, Ask:

  • “What could go wrong for the buyer?”
    • Parts availability?
    • Service support?
    • Operator skill?
  • “Am I overtrusting my own idea?”
    • Overconfidence & confirmation bias are common
  • “If this deal fails later, what will client blame us for?”
    • Keep that risk on your radar and address upfront

Slide 13: GST & % – Fast Mental Math for Sales

Quick Tricks for Indian GST (18% / 28%)

  • 18% GST trick:
    • 10% = move decimal one place left
    • 5% = half of 10%
    • 3% ≈ one‑third of 10%
  • Example: Basic price = ₹10,00,000; GST 18%
    • 10% = 1,00,000
    • 5% = 50,000
    • 3% ≈ 30,000
    • Total GST ≈ 1,80,000 → Invoice ≈ 11,80,000
  • 28% GST (if needed):
    • 30% of 10,00,000 = 3,00,000
    • minus 2% (20,000) = 2,80,000

Discount + GST Quick Check

  • If client asks 5% discount on 10,00,000:
    • 5% = 50,000 → new basic = 9,50,000
    • GST 18% ≈ 1,71,000 (18% of 9.5L)
    • Approx invoice ≈ 11,21,000
  • Train team to do ballpark mental math so they look confident on calls

Slide 14: “Seeking Wisdom” – Avoiding Sales Misjudgments

4 Common Thinking Errors from Peter Bevelin / Munger

  1. Overconfidence:
    • “Ye deal toh pakka hai” after one positive call
  2. Incentive‑caused bias:
    • Pushing wrong machine because commission is higher
  3. Commitment & consistency:
    • Sticking to a bad quote because “we already sent it”
  4. Social proof:
    • Copying other salespeople’s bad habits because “everyone does it”

Antidote:

  • Slow down, ask “And then what?” before promising anything

Slide 15: Stoic Mindset from Trekking

Sahyadri Treks → Sales Resilience

  • On a tough trek:
    • You face fear of height, fatigue, weather, sometimes real fear of death
  • After that:
    • A client saying “No” or “Your price is high” is nothing
  • Stoic idea:
    • Focus only on what you can control:
      • Number of quality calls
      • Quality of follow‑ups
      • Clarity of communication
      • Honesty and effort
    • Accept what you cannot control:
      • Market mood
      • Client’s internal politics
      • Competitor’s random discount

Slide 16: Turning Rejection into Data, Not Drama

From Emotional Reaction → Analysis

  • After lost deal, ask 3 questions:
    1. Was it price, trust, timing or product‑fit?
    2. Did the customer clearly understand our value vs alternative?
    3. What can I change in my next 10 calls based on this?
  • Treat each “No” like a failed trek route:
    • Learn, adjust, and attempt next route smarter

Slide 17: Simple Daily Routine for the Team

60–90 Minutes of “High Quality Sales Work”

  • 20–30 min:
    • Plan – top 10 prospects, WhatsApp follow‑ups, AI‑drafted proposals
  • 30–40 min:
    • Focused calling + WhatsApp with:
      • Reciprocity
      • Social proof
      • Clear next step
  • 10–15 min:
    • Post‑mortem of the day:
      • 1 thing worked, 1 mistake, 1 experiment for tomorrow

Slide 18: Key Takeaways

What I Want You to Remember

  • You don’t need deep technical knowledge to sell:
    • You need to understand people and simple numbers
  • Use:
    • Reciprocity, social proof, consistency, loss aversion in ethical ways
    • WhatsApp + AI to save time and look professional
    • Simple GST / % mental math for confidence
    • Stoic trekking mindset to digest rejection and keep going
  • Quote from Munger:
    • “It is remarkable how much long‑term advantage people like us have gotten by trying to be consistently not stupid, instead of trying to be very intelligent.”
